OVERVIEW

FA Protect is a financial advice company in Southampton. The company primarily generates leads through a cold calling structure and wanted to move away from this reliance into a digital lead generation platform through a new website.

The company had an existing website but wanted something aesthetically fresher with good SEO and a more efficient user journey.

I led the project myself collaborating with the wonderful staff at FA Protect to produce a bespoke website.

THE PROBLEM STATEMENTS

FA Protect's business model when I arrived for my first meeting was to generate potential leads through a cold calling service to offer financial advice to potential customers. The company didn't like this approach and wanted to organically produce leads through a website with good SEO and a digital marketing strategy.

The company had an existing website but it produced no leads, ranked lowly on google searches due to its poor SEO and had an ineffective user journey.

The existing website was visually outdated and lacked any real brand identity. The information architecture was poor and most of the pages were just huge blocks of text with no content priority.

The website did a poor job of illuminating the mission statement, what it did and what services it offered. All clear turn offs for potential customers and explained why the existing website was failing to capture any leads.

SWOT ANALYSIS

THE GOALS

After conducting the initial SWOT analysis on the existing website a clearly defined set of goals were established to create project boundaries.

Generate leads through the new website organically using Google search hits for financial advice.

Generate leads through targeted advertisements.

Clearly define mission statement, business services and user benefits highly in the IA.

Clearly show how the end user can get in touch with the company.

Improve UX design and navigation scheme so users can clearly find specific information.

Create an IA plan to efficiently display key information and use accordion effects to show optional supplementary information.

THE USERS

User research was very difficult with this project due to a lack of access to user data (the financial advice sector has very clearly defined rules on sharing public data). So the primary research was one on one interviews with people and looking at the limited internal data. There were two main user archetypes outlined below.

New users with no existing knowledge of what financial advice is

New users with existing knowledge of what financial advice is

USER GOALS

These are users who will more likely interact with targeted ads who are unaware of the industry before arriving on the website. These users need education of the benefits that financial advice can provide and in which services it can apply. These users will spend longer on the site before submitting a request.

These are users who have already decided they want a financial advisor and are simply comparing providers and do not require educational content about the industry. These users require a more streamlined user journey to easily submit a request.

USER RESEARCH

THE LOW FIDELITY PROTOTYPE

Without access to a userbase a low fi prototype was created without taking more steps in the user research, which was a mistake at the time and resulted in the first usability test going horribly wrong (which will be discussed further later on in the case study).

The low fi prototype focused on addressing the needs of both user archetypes. The prototype had to provide educational content for uneducated users and easily accessible submission CTA's for educated users. Designing for just one of the two would have been easy but catering to both was difficult.

The final idea was to create a landing page with company specific benefits (USP) and brief industry benefits to cater for new users but nothing too detailed that would detract from educated users experience.

More detailed industry information was then gated in the content hierarchy with CTA's on the landing page for uneducated users to learn more. This solved the existing websites issue of having all the information visible all the time.

THE USABILITY TEST

This being the first usability test in a lab I had ever conducted on my own it was a complete disaster. The tests run too long, there were navigation issues and users struggled to find specific content.

Users also stated they didn't like how "loud" the design was - there was too much colour going on and the headings of each section did not stand out enough.

WHAT DID I LEARN?

The initial concept of a hierarchical "story" guiding the users through the content as they scrolled the pages did not work at all and had to be scrapped.

The new direction needed a more minimalist aesthetic and a more robust navigation scheme.

TOBII STUDIO

Users tend to switch off after 10 minutes during a usability test, future tests would need to be short.

Most users navigate through the top navigation bar.

Users do not like reading copy - information architecture is key. Hiding secondary content behind accordions is effective.

Users respond well to conversational cues that ask questions e.g "What can we do for you?"

Finalise the user task sheet in the test clearly before the test itself.

Post test questionnaires are flimsy at best as most users forget about certain pages or functions once the test ends. Waste of time.

THE DESIGN AMENDMENTS

Clean up UI elements and remove unnecessary colour.

Increase contrast of CTA's.

Make navigation scheme more robust with main focus on top navigation bar.

Re-write the copy to match the professionalism of the company brand.

Make key pages more easily accessible from the landing page.

Cut down the amount of copy and apply accordion effects to the IA.

THE HIGH FIDELITY PROTOTYPE

With the lessons learned from the first usability test the changes were implemented and V2 of the design was created. There had been about a month gap between these points as during the time I was sitting end of year exams in university and during the time I had completed 3 design projects for uni and my design skills had improved.

THE SECOND USABILITY TEST

The second usability test was conducted with my mentor Muhammed Al Husban's UX team and it was a breeze. I learned so many techniques on data collection and how to accurately create a unit test with a thought out hierarchical progress.

The second test went perfectly and all users completed the tasks and gave great feedback on the new design. With V2 of the design being locked in development started.
